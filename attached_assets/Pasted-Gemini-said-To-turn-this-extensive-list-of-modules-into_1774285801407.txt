To turn this extensive list of modules into a functioning, professional workshop page via Replit, you need a prompt that tells the AI how to handle the data structure, the UI/UX design, and the logic for the booking links.

Since you are building a static site, the best approach is to ask Replit to create a JSON-driven gallery. This makes it easy for you to update prices or dates in one place without touching the HTML.

Copy and paste the prompt below into Replit (using Replit Agent or the Workspace):

The Replit Prompt
Role: You are an expert Web Developer specializing in high-end, static brand websites using HTML5, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript.

Task: Create a "Workshops & Training" page for "We Beautify Boats." The design must reflect 30 years of recreational marine expertise—professional, rugged, yet premium (The "Ontario Standard").

Core Requirements:

Header Section: Use the provided copy: "From Science to Surface... move beyond basic cleaning." Highlight the 30 years of expertise and the "Ontario Standard of Marine Preservation."

Global Format Banner: Create a visually distinct "Hybrid Learning" info bar showing the 45-min Online Theory (Google Meet) and 3-hour In-Person Practical.

Pricing Cards: Display the three-tier pricing: Online (FREE), In-Person ($250 CAD), and Full-Time Crew (FREE).

The Course Grid:

Organize the workshops into three distinct sections: 100 Series (Fundamentals), 200 Series (Theory & Science), and 300 Series (Leadership & Advanced).

Each course must be a "Card" featuring the Course Number, Tagline, and a "Read More" toggle for the Description.

Booking Logic: Every card needs two clear buttons: "Book Online Theory" and "Book In-Person Practical." These should link to a placeholder URL (e.g., booking.html?course=101&type=online).

Level 10 (L10) Integration: Create a reusable "L10 Meeting Structure" component or modal that explains the 6-step Kaizen process (Segue to Conclude) used in every workshop.

Footer/Legal: Include the Mandatory Recording Disclaimer in a high-visibility box near the booking buttons or in the footer.

Technical Specs:

Use Tailwind CSS for a clean, maritime-inspired aesthetic (Navy Blues, Slate Grays, Crisp Whites).

Store all course data (101 through 310) in a JavaScript Constant (JSON array) so the page renders dynamically.

Ensure the layout is mobile-responsive for marina staff checking the site on the docks.

Data to Include:
[Paste the 100, 200, and 300 series list from your message here]

Why this works for you:
Scalability: By asking for a "JSON-driven" build, if you decide to change the price of the 200 series later, you change one number in the code and it updates all 6 courses instantly.

The L10 Hook: It treats the Level 10 meeting as a "feature" of the brand, showing potential attendees that this isn't just a cleaning lesson—it's a professional management system.

Conversion Focused: Instead of one generic "Contact Us" button, each of the 22+ modules gets its own specific booking intent.
